# Forbes Court Reporting Product Brief

## What This Site Does

Forbes Court Reporting Services, LLC. needs a modern, trustworthy website that helps legal clients schedule court reporting work and helps reporters or court-reporting students understand career and internship opportunities.

The site must preserve the existing Forbes facts, tone, and family credibility while making the experience easier to scan, navigate, and act on.

## Primary Audiences

- Attorneys, law firms, courts, municipalities, agencies, and organizations that need accurate stenographic or voice court reporting.
- Experienced reporters and new graduates looking for work.
- Stenography and voice reporting students looking for courtroom exposure before graduation.

## Tone

Established, accurate, regional, calm, professional, and family-owned. The language should feel direct and reliable, not flashy or startup-like.

## Anti-References

- Generic AI SaaS landing pages.
- Purple gradients, glassmorphism, shader heroes, or fake dashboard mockups.
- Gavel and courthouse stock-photo cliches.
- Overdramatic legal imagery.
- Fake metrics, fake testimonials, fake clients, or invented certifications.
- Replacing the Forbes family story with generic corporate positioning.

## Brand Truths To Preserve

- Forbes has served Western New York and the Finger Lakes since 1972.
- Forbes creates an honest record in and out of the courtroom.
- Forbes covers stenographic and voice reporting.
- Forbes serves trials, grand juries, depositions, hearings, town and village courts, federal court, board meetings, zoning, planning, historical meetings, and public hearings.
- Forbes offers hard-copy and e-copy transcripts.
- The business is rooted in the work of Edith Forbes, Kelly Forbes, Macrae Forbes, and Edna Macrae.

## Design Goal

Modern legal heritage: structured, legible, restrained, and premium without losing the practical regional service feel.
